## NOMENCLATURE CONVENTIONS

### Article Identifiers
```
Format: AXIOM-[Number]-[Sub-number]-[Article Number]
Example: AXIOM-I-01-01 (Axiom I, Section 01, Article 01)
```

### Chapter Identifiers
```
Format: CHAPTER-[Part]-[Number]
Example: CHAPTER-I-01 (Part I, Chapter 01)
```

### Section Identifiers
```
Format: SECTION-[Code]-[Number]
Example: SECTION-NORM-01
```

## FUNDAMENTAL TERMS

### A

#### Autonomous Intelligent Agent (AIA)
**Formal Definition**: Computer system capable of perceiving its environment, making decisions, and acting autonomously without direct human intervention, using machine learning or artificial intelligence algorithms.

**Context**: Axiom I (Human Supremacy)

---

#### Audit Trail
**Formal Definition**: Immutable and chronological record of all actions, decisions, and modifications performed by an autonomous agent, enabling complete traceability and compliance verification.

**Context**: Axiom VI (Audit)

---

#### Supreme Human Authority
**Formal Definition**: Principle according to which humans retain final and irrevocable authority over critical decisions, with the capacity to intervene, modify, or stop autonomous agent actions at any time.

**Context**: Axiom I (Human Supremacy)

---

### C

#### Compliance
**Formal Definition**: State in which an autonomous agent fully respects all axioms, articles, and specifications of LAIRM, as verified by independent audit mechanisms.

**Context**: Axiom III (Responsibility)

---

#### Global Agentive Constitution
**Formal Definition**: International legislative framework establishing principles, norms, and governance mechanisms for autonomous intelligent agents, valid for the period 2026-2036.

**Context**: General LAIRM framework

---

#### Critical Decision
**Formal Definition**: Any action or decision by an autonomous agent likely to significantly affect the rights, safety, or well-being of one or more persons, requiring direct human supervision.

**Context**: Axiom I (Human Supremacy)

---

### D

#### Decentralized Digital Identity (DID)
**Formal Definition**: Unique and verifiable identifier for an autonomous agent, based on blockchain technologies, enabling authentication and traceability without depending on a centralized authority.

**Context**: Axiom II (Identity)

---

### G

#### Agentive Governance
**Formal Definition**: Set of mechanisms, processes, and institutions enabling supervision, control, and regulation of autonomous intelligent agent behavior in compliance with ethical and legal norms.

**Context**: Axiom IX (Governance)

---

### I

#### Interoperability
**Formal Definition**: Capacity of two or more autonomous agents to communicate, exchange data, and coordinate their actions transparently and securely, independently of their respective architectures or providers.

**Context**: Axiom V (Interoperability)

---

### K

#### Universal Kill Switch
**Formal Definition**: Centralized control mechanism enabling immediate and irrevocable shutdown of all autonomous agents or a specific category of agents, activatable by a competent human authority in case of existential threat.

**Context**: Axiom I (Human Supremacy)

---

### L

#### LAIRM
**Formal Definition**: Acronym for "Legislature for Autonomous Intelligent Resources Management" - international legislative framework for autonomous intelligent agent governance.

**Context**: General framework

---

### M

#### Control Mechanism
**Formal Definition**: Technical or organizational system enabling supervision, verification, and control of autonomous agent behavior, including audits, compliance tests, and emergency interventions.

**Context**: Axiom I (Human Supremacy)

---

### R

#### Agentive Responsibility
**Formal Definition**: Legal and ethical obligation of an autonomous agent to be accountable for its actions, respect established norms, and compensate for damages caused by its decisions or actions.

**Context**: Axiom III (Responsibility)

---

### S

#### Continuous Supervision
**Formal Definition**: Permanent process of monitoring and verifying autonomous agent actions by technical systems or human operators, enabling real-time detection and correction of compliance deviations.

**Context**: Axiom IV (Supervision)

---

### T

#### Complete Traceability
**Formal Definition**: Capacity to document and retrace each stage of an autonomous agent's lifecycle, from creation to deactivation, including all modifications, decisions, and interactions.

**Context**: Axiom II (Identity)

---

### V

#### Compliance Verification
**Formal Definition**: Independent and rigorous audit process enabling confirmation that an autonomous agent fully respects all LAIRM axioms and articles, with documentation of results.

**Context**: Axiom VI (Audit)
