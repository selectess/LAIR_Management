## Methodological Notes

1. **Citation format**: References follow APA 7th edition style for academic publications, with adaptations for technical and institutional documents.

2. **Unique identifiers**: Each reference has a unique identifier in brackets (e.g., [TURING-1950]) used for citations throughout the LAIRM corpus. The format is [AUTHOR-YEAR] or [PROJECT-YEAR].

3. **Accessibility**: URLs are provided when documents are accessible online. Last verification: March 2026.

4. **Evolution**: This bibliography is regularly updated to reflect new relevant publications.

5. **Categorization**: References are organized by theme to facilitate navigation:
   - Theoretical foundations (1950-1980)
   - Machine learning and neural networks (1982-2014)
   - Transformers and language models (2017-2020)
   - Classical autonomous agents (1995-2008)
   - Modern autonomous agents (2023-2026)
   - Safety and alignment
   - Ethics and governance
   - Regulatory frameworks
   - Space technologies
   - Real-world incidents and case studies (verified)
   - Blockchain and DAOs
   - Economics and societal impact

6. **Primary sources**: Priority given to peer-reviewed academic publications, official institutional reports, and reference technical documentation.

7. **Real-World Incidents**: All incident case studies are based on verified official investigations (SEC, NTSB, Congressional reports) with publicly available documentation.
