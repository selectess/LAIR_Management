# GLOSSARY
## LAIRM Technical and Legal Terminology

This glossary establishes the canonical terminology used throughout the LAIRM corpus. To ensure terminological consistency, the terms defined below constitute the reference forms to be used in all documents.

---

## A

**A2A (Agent-to-Agent Protocol)**: Standardized communication protocol for interaction between autonomous agents, co-developed by Google and OpenAI to facilitate interoperability in multi-agent systems.

**Aequitas**: Open-source algorithmic fairness audit library enabling detection and measurement of biases in machine learning systems.

**Autonomous agent**: Computer system capable of perceiving its environment, making decisions, and executing actions without continuous human intervention. Distinguished from a simple program by its adaptation capacity and perception-decision-action loop.

**Agentic (era)**: Historical period characterized by the massive deployment of autonomous agents in the real economy, beginning approximately in 2025 with the emergence of frameworks like Auto-GPT.

**AIML (Artificial Intelligence Markup Language)**: XML markup language used to create rule-based chatbots using pattern matching.

**AlexNet**: Convolutional neural network developed by Krizhevsky, Sutskever, and Hinton (2012), marking the beginning of the deep learning revolution with its victory in the ImageNet competition.

**AlphaGo**: Artificial intelligence system developed by DeepMind (2016) that defeated world Go champion Lee Sedol, demonstrating the capacity of reinforcement learning systems to master complex domains.

**Reinforcement Learning**: Machine learning paradigm where an agent learns to make decisions by maximizing cumulative reward through interaction with its environment.

**ARAM (Autonomous Resources Allocation Management)**: Open-source technical framework precursor to LAIRM, providing implementation tools for autonomous agent management (Agent Passport, kill-switch, MCP/A2A protocols).

**Arbitrum**: Layer 2 scaling solution for Ethereum using Optimistic Rollup technology, enabling faster and less expensive transactions for decentralized applications.

**Transformer Architecture**: Neural network architecture introduced by Vaswani et al. (2017) based on attention mechanisms, constituting the foundation of all modern large language models.

**Auto-GPT**: First mainstream autonomous agent framework (April 2025), enabling a language model to decompose objectives into subtasks and execute them iteratively.

**AutoGen**: Microsoft framework (2025) for creating multi-agent conversations enabling collaborative resolution of complex problems.

**Axiom**: In the LAIRM context, fundamental normative principle serving as the basis for the legislative corpus. LAIRM comprises 19 axioms (Ψ-I to Ψ-XIX).

## B

**Backpropagation**: Learning algorithm for neural networks enabling efficient calculation of gradients necessary for network weight optimization.

**BabyAGI**: Autonomous agent framework (April 2025) developed by Yohei Nakajima, distinguished by its dynamic task prioritization capability.

**BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)**: Bidirectional language model developed by Google (2018), revolutionizing natural language understanding.

**Big Data**: Data sets whose volume, velocity, and variety exceed the capabilities of traditional data management tools.

**Blockchain**: Distributed ledger technology enabling immutable and transparent recording of transactions, used notably for implementing audit mechanisms in LAIRM.

## C

**C4ISR**: Military acronym designating Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance. Systems increasingly integrating autonomous agents for military coordination.

**CCAC (Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons)**: United Nations international treaty regulating the use of certain conventional weapons, currently under debate to include Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems (LAWS).

**Chainlink**: Decentralized oracle network enabling blockchain smart contracts to securely access external data, APIs, and payment systems.

**Chatbot**: Conversational agent capable of interacting with human users via natural language. Distinction between rule-based chatbots (AIML) and language model-based chatbots (LLM).

**ChatGPT**: Conversational interface developed by OpenAI (November 2022) based on GPT-3.5 then GPT-4, reaching 100 million users in 2 months and democratizing access to language models.

**Claude**: Family of language models developed by Anthropic (2023-2026), designed with emphasis on safety and value alignment.

**CLPS (Commercial Lunar Payload Services)**: NASA commercial lunar missions program using autonomous rovers and agents for exploration and scientific research.

**CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)**: Neural network architecture specialized in image processing, using convolution operations to extract hierarchical features.

**Connectionism**: Approach to artificial intelligence based on artificial neural networks, as opposed to symbolic approaches.

**CrewAI**: Multi-agent orchestration framework (2025) enabling creation of agentic hierarchies with specialized roles.

**Cybernetics**: Science of control and communication in complex systems, founded by Norbert Wiener (1948), constituting a theoretical foundation of LAIRM.

## D

**DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)**: Decentralized autonomous organization, blockchain-based governance structure where decisions are made by algorithmic consensus. Governed by Axiom Ψ-IX.5 (GUBERNATIO DECENTRALIZATA) in LAIRM.

**Deadlock**: Blocking situation in a multi-agent system where multiple agents mutually await resources or decisions, creating an infinite loop without automatic resolution.

**Deep Blue**: Artificial intelligence system developed by IBM that defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov in 1997.

**Deep Learning**: Subfield of machine learning using deep neural networks (multiple layers) to learn hierarchical data representations.

**DID (Decentralized Identifier)**: Decentralized identifier conforming to W3C standard, used in LAIRM for portable and verifiable identity of autonomous agents (Axiom Ψ-II IDENTITAS AGENTICA).

## E

**Edge Computing**: Distributed computing paradigm where data processing occurs near its source rather than in centralized data centers, enabling reduced latency for autonomous agents.

**ELIZA**: First chatbot developed by Joseph Weizenbaum (1966), simulating a Rogerian therapist through pattern matching.

**Embedding**: Dense vector representation of data (text, images, etc.) in a reduced-dimension space, enabling language models to capture semantic relationships.

**Algorithmic escalation**: Risk of uncontrolled escalation in interactions between autonomous agents, particularly critical in military contexts where rapid decisions can lead to conflicts.

**Ethereum**: Decentralized blockchain platform enabling smart contract execution, used as infrastructure for DAOs and LAIRM immutable audit mechanisms.

**EU AI Act**: European regulation on artificial intelligence, first comprehensive regulatory framework for AI, adopted in 2025.

## F

**Fairlearn**: Microsoft open-source Python library for evaluating and mitigating biases in machine learning models, used in Axiom Ψ-VIII (ETHICA PROGRAMMATA).

**Few-shot learning**: Ability of a model to learn new tasks from a limited number of examples provided in the context (prompt).

**Fine-tuning**: Process of adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task through additional training on targeted data.

**Flash crash**: Rapid and brutal collapse of financial markets caused by automated trading algorithms, illustrating systemic risks of unregulated autonomous agents.

**Frame problem**: Philosophical and technical problem concerning the representation of change in a dynamic environment, identified in the 1970s.

**Function calling**: Ability of language models to invoke external functions or APIs in a structured manner, technical foundation of modern autonomous agents.

## G

**GAN (Generative Adversarial Network)**: Neural network architecture introduced by Ian Goodfellow (2014) where two networks (generator and discriminator) compete to produce realistic synthetic data.

**Gnosis Safe**: Multi-signature wallet solution for Ethereum, used in LAIRM-compliant DAOs to guarantee collective human validation of critical decisions.

**GPAI (Global Partnership on AI)**: Global Partnership on Artificial Intelligence, multilateral initiative promoting responsible AI development, identified as a vector for LAIRM adoption.

**GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)**: Family of language models developed by OpenAI, including GPT-1 (2018), GPT-2 (2019), GPT-3 (2020), GPT-4 (2023).

**GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**: Graphics processor whose parallel architecture has proven particularly suited to training deep neural networks.

## H

**Hallucination**: Phenomenon where a language model generates factually incorrect or invented information with an appearance of confidence, representing a major risk for autonomous agents making critical decisions.

**AI winter**: Periods of drastic reduction in funding and interest for artificial intelligence research, notably 1974-1980 and 1987-1993.

**Hopfield Network**: Type of recurrent neural network proposed by John Hopfield (1982), functioning as an associative memory.

## I

**ImageNet**: Database of 14 million annotated images created by Fei-Fei Li (2009), having catalyzed the deep learning revolution.

**Interoperability**: Ability of heterogeneous systems to exchange information and cooperate effectively. Constitutes Axiom Ψ-V of LAIRM.

**IPFS (InterPlanetary File System)**: Peer-to-peer distributed file system enabling decentralized and immutable data storage, used for archiving DAO constitutions and LAIRM audits.

**ISO (International Organization for Standardization)**: International standardization organization, identified as potential vector for LAIRM standards adoption via ISO 42001 standard on AI management systems.

## K

**Kill-switch**: Emergency deactivation mechanism enabling immediate shutdown of an autonomous agent in less than 500 milliseconds. Central element of Axiom Ψ-I (SUPREMATIA HUMANA), implemented via three redundant channels (network, local, hardware).

**Kubernetes**: Open-source container orchestration system used for large-scale deployment and management of autonomous agents in production environments.

## L

**LAIRM (Legislature for Autonomous Intelligent Resources Management)**: International legislative corpus for autonomous agent governance, structured in 19 axioms and 361 articles.

**LangChain**: Open-source framework (2022-2025) for developing language model-based applications, having become the de facto standard for agent creation.

**LangGraph**: LangChain extension (January 2025) enabling creation of complex agentic workflows via state graphs with persistent memory and conditional decision points.

**LAWS (Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems)**: Lethal autonomous weapons systems capable of selecting and engaging targets without human intervention. Prohibited by Axiom Ψ-XI (ARMA PROHIBITA) of LAIRM.

**Layer 2**: Blockchain scaling solutions built atop a main blockchain (Layer 1) to improve throughput and reduce transaction costs, used for LAIRM-compliant DAOs.

**Ledger**: Immutable and distributed register recording all transactions and decisions of a system, foundation of Axiom Ψ-VI (AUDITUM IMMUTABILE) for agent traceability.

**LISP (List Processing)**: Programming language created by John McCarthy (1958), historically associated with artificial intelligence research.

**LLM (Large Language Model)**: Large-scale language model (typically >1 billion parameters) pre-trained on massive text corpora.

**LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)**: Efficient language model adaptation technique enabling fine-tuning with reduced computational resources.

## M

**Machine Learning**: Subfield of artificial intelligence where systems learn from data without being explicitly programmed for each task.

**MCP (Model Context Protocol)**: Standardized protocol developed by Anthropic (November 2025) for connection between autonomous agents and external tools, having become the de facto standard with 2,500+ registered MCP servers in March 2026.

**Multi-agent system**: System composed of multiple autonomous agents interacting to solve complex problems or achieve common objectives.

**Multi-sig (Multi-signature)**: Blockchain security mechanism requiring multiple cryptographic signatures to authorize a transaction, mandatory in LAIRM-compliant DAOs (Article Ψ-IX.5.1).

**MYCIN**: Expert system developed at Stanford (1976) for medical diagnosis, demonstrating that AI could achieve performance comparable to human experts in specialized domains.

## N

**NIST AI RMF (Risk Management Framework)**: Artificial intelligence risk management framework developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (United States).

**Autonomy level**: Classification of agents according to their degree of decisional autonomy, from Level 1 (reactive, fixed rules) to Level 5 (near-human autonomy).

## O

**ONNX (Open Neural Network Exchange)**: Open format for representing machine learning models, facilitating interoperability and edge deployment of autonomous agents.

**Optimism**: Layer 2 solution for Ethereum using Optimistic Rollup technology, enabling fast and economical transactions for decentralized applications and DAOs.

**Human override**: Capability for a human operator to intervene and immediately modify an autonomous agent's decisions without prior justification. Fundamental principle of Axiom Ψ-I (SUPREMATIA HUMANA).

## P

**PageRank**: Algorithm developed by Larry Page and Sergey Brin (1998) to rank web pages, constituting Google's foundation and an early example of large-scale AI.

**Perceptron**: First artificial neural network model developed by Frank Rosenblatt (1958).

**Prompt engineering**: Discipline consisting of formulating optimal instructions to obtain desired results from a language model.

**Property-based testing**: Software testing methodology verifying that general properties are satisfied for a wide range of automatically generated inputs.

## Q

**QLoRA (Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation)**: Advanced language model fine-tuning technique combining quantization and low-rank adaptation for maximum memory efficiency.

**Quadratic Voting**: Voting mechanism where the cost of an additional vote increases quadratically, used in LAIRM-compliant DAOs to limit plutocracy (Article Ψ-IX.5.6).

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: Technique combining information retrieval and text generation, enabling autonomous agents to access external knowledge bases to improve their responses.

**Responsibility cascade**: Legal responsibility distribution model proposed by LAIRM (Axiom Ψ-III) according to a 40/40/20 ratio between model creator, agent deployer, and human supervisor.

**Neural network**: Computational model inspired by biological brain functioning, composed of artificial neurons organized in layers.

**RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)**: Language model training technique using human feedback to align model behavior with human preferences.

**RNN (Recurrent Neural Network)**: Neural network architecture adapted to sequence processing, where connections form cycles enabling memory of previous states.

**RPA (Robotic Process Automation)**: Automation of repetitive tasks by software agents following predefined rules.

## S

**Serverless**: Cloud deployment architecture where infrastructure is automatically managed, enabling on-demand execution of autonomous agents without server management.

**Shakey**: First autonomous mobile robot developed at Stanford Research Institute (1966-1972), capable of planning and navigation.

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: Technique enabling an autonomous agent to build a map of its environment while simultaneously localizing itself, used in lunar rovers.

**Smart contract**: Self-executing program deployed on a blockchain, whose terms are directly written in code. Used for implementing DAOs and LAIRM audit mechanisms.

**Solidity**: Contract-oriented programming language for writing smart contracts on Ethereum and EVM-compatible blockchains.

**Space Computing**: Distributed computing paradigm for autonomous agents operating in orbital, lunar, or interplanetary space, with specific constraints of latency, bandwidth, and autonomy.

**Starlink**: Low Earth orbit satellite constellation developed by SpaceX, first operational orbital multi-agent system with 12,000+ satellites and 400+ autonomous management agents.

**Superintelligence**: Hypothesis of artificial intelligence significantly exceeding human cognitive capabilities in all domains.

**Continuous supervision**: Principle according to which autonomous agents must be subject to permanent monitoring with automatic escalation in case of anomaly. Element of Axiom Ψ-IV (CIRCULUS CLAUSUS).

**Support Vector Machine (SVM)**: Supervised learning algorithm used for classification and regression, popular in the 2000s.

**Swarm**: Multi-agent system where numerous simple agents cooperate to produce complex collective behaviors, presenting risks of coordinated escalation.

**Expert system**: Type of artificial intelligence system using a knowledge base and inference engine to solve problems in a specialized domain.

## T

**TensorRT**: NVIDIA library for optimization and acceleration of neural network inference, used for edge deployment of autonomous agents.

**Turing Test**: Test proposed by Alan Turing (1950) to evaluate a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human.

**Timelock**: Mandatory delay mechanism (typically 48 hours) before execution of critical decisions in DAOs, enabling review and opposition (Article Ψ-IX.5.8).

**Transformer**: See Transformer Architecture.

## U

**UNOOSA (United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs)**: United Nations office for outer space affairs, identified as potential authority for arbitration of conflicts involving spatial autonomous agents.

## V

**Normative void**: Absence of adapted legal framework to regulate an emerging phenomenon. LAIRM aims to fill the normative void concerning autonomous agents, as no global jurisdiction possesses specific "agentic" regulation in March 2026.

## W

**Web crawler**: Software agent automatically traversing the World Wide Web to index content.

**Web3**: Decentralized vision of the Internet based on blockchain, cryptocurrencies, and peer-to-peer technologies, infrastructure for DAOs and decentralized agent identity.

## Ψ

**Ψ (Psi)**: Symbol used to designate LAIRM axioms (Ψ-I to Ψ-XIX), chosen for its connotation of fundamental principle in sciences.

## Usage Notes

1. **Terminological consistency**: Terms defined in this glossary constitute the canonical forms to be used in all LAIRM documents.

2. **Discouraged synonyms**: 
   - Prefer "autonomous agent" to "agentic AI", "autonomous system", "intelligent agent"
   - Prefer "language model" to "LLM" in formal texts (except technical context)
   - Maintain English technical terms in English-language documents

3. **Evolution**: This glossary is a living document, intended to be enriched as the LAIRM corpus evolves.

4. **References**: For detailed technical definitions, consult corresponding chapters of the Reference Compendium.
