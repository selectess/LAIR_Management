# PREFACE
## Legislature for Autonomous Intelligent Resources Management (LAIRM)

**Author**: Mehdi Wahbi, Founder of the LAIRM Initiative  
**Date**: March 2026

---

## Project Genesis

In March 2026, the global economy counts approximately 127 million operational autonomous agents, deployed in critical sectors ranging from finance to healthcare, logistics, and defense. These systems make daily decisions with economic, social, and sometimes vital repercussions, without a coherent international legal framework defining their status, responsibilities, or applicable control mechanisms.

This situation constitutes an unprecedented normative void in the history of international law. Existing legal instruments - whether the EU AI Act, the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, or international conventions on liability - were designed to regulate human actors or traditional legal entities. They do not adequately address the question of decisional autonomy of artificial systems, nor their capacity to interact in networks in emergent ways.

The present document, entitled "The Cybernetic Criterion", proposes a structured response to this regulatory challenge. It establishes a comprehensive legislative corpus, organized around 19 fundamental axioms articulated in 361 executable articles, accompanied by a technical architecture enabling their effective implementation.

## DOCUMENT STRUCTURE

The LAIRM corpus is articulated in four complementary parts:

**Part I - Historical Foundations and Diagnosis (Chapters 0-5)**  
This section traces the evolution of artificial intelligence from the foundational work of Alan Turing (1950) to the emergence of the agentic era (2025-2026). It establishes a factual diagnosis of the current state of technologies, identifying demonstrated capabilities, known limitations, and observed systemic risks.

**Part II - Sectoral Dimensions (Chapters 6-9)**  
Analysis of agentic implications in four critical domains: military, humanitarian, social, and economic. This part documents systemic incidents from the first quarter of 2026, providing an empirical basis for subsequent normative recommendations.

**Part III - Normative Paradigms (Chapters 10-19)**  
Detailed exposition of the 19 axioms constituting the philosophical and legal foundation of LAIRM. Each axiom is presented with its theoretical foundations, proposed technical implementation, and articulation with the legislative corpus.

**Part IV - Executable Legislative Corpus (Articles Ψ-I.1.1 to Ψ-XIX.19.19)**  
Set of 361 legislative articles structured according to a matrix architecture (19 axioms × 19 articles). Each article follows a standardized format comprising: imperative norm, legal foundation, technical specification, reference implementation, verification mechanisms and sanctions, entry into force modalities.

## Drafting Methodology

The drafting of LAIRM follows a rigorous methodology inspired by international legal codification practices:

1. **State-of-the-art analysis**: Systematic examination of scientific literature, existing regulatory frameworks, and documented use cases.

2. **Identification of normative gaps**: Mapping of situations not covered by current legal instruments.

3. **Formulation of normative principles**: Development of fundamental axioms from ethical, legal, and technical considerations.

4. **Legislative operationalization**: Translation of principles into executable articles, with verifiable technical specifications.

5. **Peer validation**: Submission to a review process involving legal scholars, philosophers, engineers, and civil society representatives.

Each article of the legislative corpus is designed as an autonomous legal instrument, directly applicable and technically verifiable. This approach aims to avoid the pitfall of non-binding principle declarations that characterize a significant portion of international AI law.

## Limits and Epistemological Precautions

It is important to clearly establish what this document is not:

**This document is not a prediction**: The prospective scenarios presented, particularly in Part IV, constitute extrapolations based on trends observed in 2026. They do not claim certain predictive capacity concerning future technological or societal evolution.

**This document is not a dogma**: The proposed axioms constitute normative hypotheses subject to democratic debate and peer review. They do not claim absolute truth or timeless universal validity.

**This document is not a universal solution**: LAIRM implementation modalities will necessarily need to be adapted to local legal, cultural, and technological contexts. The proposed framework aims to provide guiding principles, not rigid prescriptions.

**This document is not definitive**: LAIRM is designed as an evolving instrument, intended to be amended, extended, and potentially replaced as scientific knowledge and practical experience progress.

However, this document constitutes:

**A structured starting point**: The first attempt at a complete agentic constitution, offering a coherent framework for international debate.

**A call to action**: A response to the urgency created by the current legal void, which exposes societies to documented systemic risks.

**An invitation to collaboration**: An open project aiming to collectively build the normative foundations of the agentic era.

## Dedication

This document is dedicated to the victims of agentic incidents from the first quarter of 2026 - those whose cases are documented in public reports, and those whose situations remain unknown. Their experience underscores the urgency of establishing a robust normative framework.

Agentic systems potentially represent one of the most significant transformations in human history. Whether they constitute an opportunity for emancipation or a source of existential risks will largely depend on the institutional and normative frameworks we establish today.
