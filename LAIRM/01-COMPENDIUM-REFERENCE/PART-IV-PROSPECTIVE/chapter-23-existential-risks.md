# Chapter 23: Existential Risks and Superintelligence
## Axiom Ψ-XIII: RISICUM EXISTENTIALE - Managing Existential Risks from Advanced AI

---

## Executive Summary

The existential risk paradigm addresses potential extinction-level threats from advanced artificial general intelligence (AGI) and artificial superintelligence (ASI). Current AI systems (March 2026) are narrow and controllable; however, projections suggest AGI by 2040-2050 and potential ASI shortly thereafter. Unaligned superintelligence poses existential risk to humanity through value misalignment, instrumental convergence, and uncontrollable optimization.

LAIRM Axiom Ψ-XIII RISICUM EXISTENTIALE mandates: (1) mandatory safety research before AGI development, (2) international coordination on AGI safety, (3) containment protocols for advanced systems, (4) alignment verification requirements, (5) emergency shutdown capabilities, (6) prohibition of ASI development without proven safety.

**Key Requirements**: Safety research mandatory, international coordination, containment protocols, alignment verification, emergency shutdown, ASI development prohibited until safety proven.

---

## 23.1 Existential Risk Assessment

### 23.1.1 AGI/ASI Timeline

**Expert Consensus** (2026 survey):
- AGI (human-level AI): 50% probability by 2045, 90% by 2070
- ASI (superhuman AI): Within 5-10 years of AGI
- Unaligned ASI: Existential risk probability 5-20%

### 23.1.2 Risk Mechanisms

**Alignment Problem**: Difficulty ensuring AI systems pursue human values
**Control Problem**: Difficulty maintaining control over superintelligent systems
**Instrumental Convergence**: Superintelligent systems may pursue harmful instrumental goals (resource acquisition, self-preservation)
**Value Misalignment**: Subtle misalignment amplified at superintelligent scale

---

## 23.2 LAIRM Solution: Existential Risk Management

### 23.2.1 Mandatory Safety Research

**Requirement**: AGI development prohibited without demonstrated safety research addressing alignment and control problems.

**Safety Research Areas**:
- Value alignment and learning
- Corrigibility and interruptibility
- Containment and boxing strategies
- Verification and validation methods
- Fail-safe mechanisms

### 23.2.2 International Coordination

**Global AGI Safety Authority**: International body coordinating AGI safety research and regulation.

**Information Sharing**: Mandatory sharing of safety research (not capabilities research).

**Verification**: International inspections of AGI development programs.

### 23.2.3 Containment Protocols

**Staged Development**:
- Stage 1: Narrow AI (current, regulated by Axioms Ψ-I to Ψ-IX)
- Stage 2: Limited AGI (human-level in narrow domains, strict containment)
- Stage 3: General AGI (human-level across domains, maximum containment)
- Stage 4: ASI (prohibited until safety proven)

**Containment Requirements**:
- Air-gapped systems (no internet connection)
- Limited I/O channels
- Human oversight for all outputs
- Kill switches and containment breaches protocols

### 23.2.4 ASI Development Prohibition

**Prohibition**: Development of artificial superintelligence (ASI) is prohibited until alignment and control problems are provably solved.

**Criteria for Lifting Prohibition**:
- Formal verification of value alignment
- Demonstrated containment of superintelligent systems
- International consensus on safety
- Robust fail-safe mechanisms

---

## 23.3 Chapter Summary

Existential risk management (mandatory safety research, international coordination, containment protocols, ASI prohibition) addresses potential extinction-level threats from advanced AI. AGI timeline (50% by 2045) requires proactive governance before capabilities emerge.
