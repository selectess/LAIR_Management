# Chapter 24: Geoeconomic Justice and Global Inequality
## Axiom Ψ-XIV: IUSTITIA MUNDANA - Global Economic Justice in the Agentic Era

---

## Executive Summary

The geoeconomic justice paradigm addresses how agentic technology exacerbates the North-South divide and global inequality. Current trajectory (March 2026) shows 95% of agentic market value concentrated in developed countries, with developing countries unable to access or afford agentic systems. Without intervention, this creates permanent technological apartheid, with developing countries perpetually dependent on developed nations for agentic capabilities.

LAIRM Axiom Ψ-XIV IUSTITIA MUNDANA mandates: (1) technology transfer to developing countries, (2) capacity building programs, (3) equitable access mechanisms, (4) just transition support, (5) open-source agentic platforms, (6) global redistribution of agentic productivity gains.

**Key Requirements**: Technology transfer mandatory, capacity building funded, equitable access ensured, just transition supported, open-source platforms, global redistribution.

---

## 24.1 Current State: Global Inequality (March 2026)

### 24.1.1 Geographic Concentration

**Agentic Market Distribution**:
- North America: 45% ($2.7B)
- Europe: 28% ($1.7B)
- Asia-Pacific (developed): 22% ($1.3B)
- Rest of World: 5% ($0.3B)

**Critical Finding**: 95% of agentic value concentrated in developed countries, 5% in developing countries (home to 85% of global population).

### 24.1.2 Access Barriers

**Cost Barriers**: Average agent costs $50,000/year, exceeding annual GDP per capita in 47 countries.

**Infrastructure Barriers**: 3.7 billion people lack broadband access required for agentic systems.

**Skills Barriers**: 4.2 billion people lack digital literacy for agentic interaction.

**Language Barriers**: 89% of agents English-centric, excluding 5.1 billion non-English speakers.

---

## 24.2 LAIRM Solution: Global Justice Framework

### 24.2.1 Technology Transfer

**Mandatory Transfer**: Developed countries must transfer agentic technology to developing countries.

**Mechanisms**:
- Open-source agentic platforms
- Patent sharing and licensing
- Technical documentation and training
- Infrastructure support

### 24.2.2 Capacity Building

**Global Fund**: $100 billion/year for developing country capacity building.

**Programs**:
- Technical training (1 million developers/year)
- Infrastructure development (broadband, data centers)
- Research institutions (100 AI research centers in developing countries)
- Educational programs (AI literacy for 100 million people/year)

### 24.2.3 Equitable Access

**Subsidized Access**: Developing countries receive 90% subsidized access to agentic systems.

**Open-Source Platforms**: Mandatory open-source alternatives for all critical agentic capabilities.

**Local Ownership**: Developing countries retain ownership of locally-adapted agents.

### 24.2.4 Just Transition Support

**Transition Fund**: $50 billion/year for developing countries affected by agentic displacement.

**Support**:
- Transition income (80% wage for 24 months)
- Retraining programs
- Social protection
- Economic diversification

---

## 24.3 Chapter Summary

Geoeconomic justice (technology transfer, capacity building, equitable access, just transition) prevents permanent technological apartheid. Current concentration (95% in developed countries) requires immediate global redistribution mechanisms.
