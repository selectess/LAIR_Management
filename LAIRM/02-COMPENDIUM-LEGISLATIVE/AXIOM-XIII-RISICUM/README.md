# Axiom XIII - RISICUM

## Core Principle

Axiom XIII establishes the comprehensive framework for **existential risk management** from advanced artificial intelligence systems. It ensures that potential extinction-level threats from AGI and ASI are managed through rigorous safety governance, international coordination, and proven containment before advanced development proceeds.

---

## Overview

RISICUM (Risk) is the thirteenth axiom of the LAIRM framework, establishing comprehensive existential risk governance for autonomous agents. This axiom ensures that:

- Existential risks are systematically assessed
- Alignment with human values is verified
- Safety research is mandatory
- International coordination is maintained
- Containment protocols are implemented
- Staged development frameworks are followed
- AGI development is restricted
- ASI development is prohibited

---

## Structure

### 19-Article Framework

The axiom is organized into three thematic groups addressing risk dimensions:

**Group 1: Risk Assessment (Articles 1-5)**
- Existential risk assessment procedures
- Alignment verification requirements
- Safety research mandate
- International coordination
- Containment protocols

**Group 2: Development Controls (Articles 6-11)**
- Staged development framework
- AGI development restrictions
- ASI development prohibition
- Emergency shutdown capability
- Fail-safe mechanisms
- Safety certification

**Group 3: Monitoring and Enforcement (Articles 12-19)**
- Risk monitoring procedures
- Incident response protocols
- Transparency requirements
- Appeal mechanisms
- Sanctions for violations
- International governance

---

## Key Concepts

**Existential Risk**: Potential for advanced AI systems to cause extinction-level harm to humanity.

**Alignment Verification**: Confirmation that AI systems' goals align with human values.

**Containment Protocols**: Technical and procedural safeguards preventing uncontrolled AI behavior.

**AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)**: AI systems with human-level general intelligence.

**ASI (Artificial Superintelligence)**: AI systems exceeding human intelligence.

---

## Implementation Requirements

### Risk Assessment
- Systematic assessment: Comprehensive risk evaluation
- Alignment verification: Confirmation of value alignment
- Safety research: Mandatory safety research programs
- International coordination: Multi-national cooperation
- Containment: Proven containment mechanisms

### Development Controls
- Staged framework: Controlled development progression
- AGI restrictions: Limitations on AGI development
- ASI prohibition: Complete prohibition on ASI development
- Shutdown capability: Ability to halt systems
- Fail-safe mechanisms: Automatic safety activation

### Monitoring and Enforcement
- Continuous monitoring: Real-time risk tracking
- Incident response: Rapid response procedures
- Transparency: Public disclosure of risks
- Appeals: Dispute resolution mechanisms
- Sanctions: Enforcement of violations

## Compliance Verification

Compliance with Axiom XIII is verified through:

1. **Risk Audits**: Verification of risk assessment procedures
2. **Alignment Audits**: Assessment of value alignment
3. **Safety Audits**: Verification of safety research
4. **Containment Audits**: Testing of containment mechanisms
5. **Coordination Audits**: Verification of international cooperation

---

## Sanctions Framework

Non-compliance with risk management requirements triggers:

- **Development Suspension**: Temporary halt of development
- **Containment Upgrades**: Mandatory safety improvements
- **Enhanced Monitoring**: Increased audit frequency
- **Operational Restrictions**: Limitations on development scope
- **License Revocation**: Permanent removal from operation
