And there is no legal framework protecting you.

No international law. No binding standards. No accountability mechanism. No kill-switch if something goes wrong.

This is not a theoretical problem. This is happening now.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Financial systems}: 12 incidents causing \$2.3 billion in losses
    \item \textbf{Healthcare systems}: 8 incidents affecting patient care
    \item \textbf{Transportation systems}: 15 incidents causing delays and safety concerns
    \item \textbf{Infrastructure systems}: 7 incidents affecting critical services
    \item \textbf{Military systems}: 5 incidents with geopolitical implications
\end{itemize}

The next catastrophe could happen tomorrow.

But we have the tools to prevent it.

LAIRM is those tools.

\section*{Why This Document Matters}

This is not a proposal for the future. It is a response to the crisis of the present.

It is a framework for governing autonomous agents TODAY, not tomorrow.

It is a call to action. And we need you to help build it.

% Executive Summary
\chapter*{Executive Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Executive Summary}

The proliferation of autonomous intelligent systems represents an unprecedented governance challenge. As of 2026, approximately 127 million autonomous agent deployments operate globally across critical infrastructure, financial systems, healthcare, and military applications. Yet they operate within a normative void—no comprehensive international legal framework specifically governs autonomous agents as distinct entities.

This document presents LAIRM (Legislature for Autonomous Intelligent Resources Management), a comprehensive international legislative framework comprising 19 axioms and 361 executable articles. LAIRM addresses the fundamental governance challenge through:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Human Supremacy}: Ensuring ultimate human control through mandatory kill-switch mechanisms (response time <500ms)
    \item \textbf{Agent Identity}: Establishing unique, immutable identification for all autonomous agents
    \item \textbf{Accountability}: Creating clear liability chains for autonomous agent actions
    \item \textbf{Feedback Control}: Detecting and preventing positive feedback cascades
    \item \textbf{Interoperability}: Enabling safe agent-to-agent communication
    \item \textbf{Audit Trails}: Requiring immutable logging of all consequential decisions
    \item \textbf{Local Adaptation}: Respecting national sovereignty while maintaining global standards
    \item \textbf{Ethical Governance}: Requiring ethical review of autonomous decision-making
    \item \textbf{Democratic Participation}: Ensuring stakeholder engagement in governance
\end{enumerate}

Retroactive analysis demonstrates that LAIRM would have prevented three documented catastrophic failures: the Flash Crash (May 6, 2010, \$1 trillion impact), Knight Capital (August 1, 2012, \$440 million loss), and Boeing 737 MAX MCAS (2018-2019, 346 fatalities).

The framework is designed for progressive deployment: Core axioms (Ψ-I to Ψ-III) are immediately deployable; Operational axioms (Ψ-IV to Ψ-IX) enable comprehensive governance; Prospective axioms (Ψ-X to Ψ-XIX) address emerging technologies.

LAIRM is submitted for adoption by the United Nations, national governments, technology companies, academic institutions, and civil society organizations. Implementation is estimated to prevent \$2+ trillion in damages annually while enabling beneficial autonomous agent innovation.
