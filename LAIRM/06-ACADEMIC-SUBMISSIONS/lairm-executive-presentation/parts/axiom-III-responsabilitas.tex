\label{ch:axiom-III}

\section*{Foundational Principle}

\begin{principlebox}
Every autonomous agent action must be traceable to responsible human actors. Responsibility is distributed according to the causal chain: developers (40\%), deployers (40\%), operators (20\%). No actor may escape accountability through claims of algorithmic opacity or emergent behavior.
\end{principlebox}

\section{Overview}

Axiom Ψ-III establishes mandatory accountability and liability frameworks. This axiom eliminates the responsibility gap by assigning clear liability to developers, deployers, and operators. It ensures that autonomous agent failures result in concrete consequences for responsible parties.

\subsection{Core Obligations}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Developers bear 40\% liability for agent design and implementation
    \item Deployers bear 40\% liability for deployment decisions and configuration
    \item Operators bear 20\% liability for operational oversight and monitoring
    \item Liability is joint and several (victims may recover from any party)
    \item No party may disclaim responsibility through algorithmic opacity
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section{Article III.1: Developer Liability}

\begin{articlebox}[Article III.1.1: Developer Responsibility]
Developers are liable for:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Design flaws and implementation errors
    \item Inadequate safety testing and validation
    \item Failure to document known limitations
    \item Inadequate kill-switch or override mechanisms
    \item Failure to implement required audit trails
\end{enumerate}

Developers MUST maintain liability insurance covering minimum €50 million per incident.
\end{articlebox}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section{Article III.2: Deployer Liability}

\begin{articlebox}[Article III.2.1: Deployer Responsibility]
Deployers are liable for:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Inappropriate deployment contexts
    \item Inadequate monitoring and oversight
    \item Failure to implement required safeguards
    \item Inadequate operator training
    \item Failure to maintain required insurance
\end{enumerate}

Deployers MUST maintain liability insurance covering minimum €50 million per incident.
\end{articlebox}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section{Article III.3: Operator Liability}

\begin{articlebox}[Article III.3.1: Operator Responsibility]
Operators are liable for:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Failure to monitor agent behavior
    \item Failure to respond to anomalies
    \item Negligent operation or configuration
    \item Failure to maintain required logs
    \item Failure to execute emergency procedures
\end{enumerate}

Operators MUST be trained and certified by regulatory authorities.
\end{articlebox}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\section{Article III.4: Victim Compensation}

\begin{articlebox}[Article III.4.1: Mandatory Compensation]
Victims of autonomous agent failures are entitled to:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Full economic damages (direct and consequential)
    \item Pain and suffering damages (if applicable)
    \item Punitive damages (if willful misconduct proven)
    \item Recovery from any liable party (joint and several liability)
    \item Priority access to liability insurance proceeds
\end{enumerate}

Compensation claims MUST be resolved within 12 months.
\end{articlebox}
