A New Constitutional Framework

Built on open-source principles, LAIR Management is the first comprehensive international legislative corpus for the governance of intelligent autonomous agents. The framework is freely available for implementation, adaptation, and contribution by open-source communities, AI and agentic system developers, civil society worldwide, governments, and organizations—fostering a truly democratic and collaborative approach to AI governance.

In March 2026, the global economy operates approximately 127 million autonomous agents across finance, healthcare, logistics, defense, energy, and governance. These systems execute decisions with economic, social, and vital repercussions—yet no coherent international legal framework defines their status, responsibilities, or control mechanisms.

LAIR Management emerges as a Global Agentive Constitution establishing the principles, norms, and control mechanisms to ensure autonomous AI systems operate in alignment with human values and the public interest. Through transparency, accessibility, and collaborative governance, we empower the global community to shape the future of AI together.

The 19 Fundamental Axioms

Interconnected Framework for AI Governance

Ψ-I

Suprematia Humana

Human supremacy and kill-switch mechanisms

Establishes absolute human authority over autonomous agents. Mandates universal kill-switch mechanisms, continuous human oversight, and the right to intervention at any moment. No autonomous system may override human decision-making authority.

Ψ-II

Identitas Agentica

Agent identity and traceability

Every autonomous agent must possess a unique, immutable digital identity. Includes cryptographic signatures, version control, and complete audit trails. Enables accountability and attribution of all agent actions.

Ψ-III

Responsabilitas

Responsibility attribution

Clear assignment of legal and operational responsibility. Defines liability chains from agent creators to deployers to operators. Ensures accountability for all autonomous system decisions and their consequences.

Ψ-IV

Circulus Clausus

Closed-loop supervision

Establishes continuous human supervision mechanisms. Agents operate within defined parameters with real-time monitoring. Human operators maintain constant awareness and intervention capability over all autonomous operations.

Ψ-V

Interoperabilitas

Interoperability standards

Defines universal standards for agent communication and coordination. Ensures compatibility across different systems, platforms, and jurisdictions. Prevents proprietary lock-in and enables transparent multi-agent ecosystems.

Ψ-VI

Auditum Immutabile

Immutable audit trails

Mandates permanent, tamper-proof records of all agent decisions and actions. Enables forensic analysis, compliance verification, and historical accountability. Creates verifiable evidence for legal proceedings and regulatory oversight.

Ψ-VII

Adaptatio Localis

Local adaptation and jurisdiction

Allows autonomous systems to adapt to local legal, cultural, and regulatory contexts. Respects national sovereignty while maintaining global governance principles. Enables implementation across diverse jurisdictions without compromising core protections.

Ψ-VIII

Ethica Programmata

Programmed ethics and values

Requires explicit encoding of ethical principles into autonomous systems. Defines universal values including human dignity, fairness, transparency, and public interest. Ensures agents operate according to shared ethical frameworks.

Ψ-IX

Gubernatio Hybrida

Hybrid human-agent governance

Establishes collaborative governance models combining human judgment with agent capabilities. Humans retain final decision authority while leveraging agent analysis and recommendations. Creates balanced systems that maximize benefits while maintaining control.

Ψ-X

Soberania Energetica

Energy sovereignty and sustainability

Addresses autonomous systems' energy consumption and environmental impact. Mandates sustainable power sources and efficiency standards. Prevents autonomous agents from destabilizing energy systems or creating environmental harm.

Ψ-XI

Arma Autonoma Prohibita

Prohibition of autonomous weapons

Establishes absolute prohibition on fully autonomous weapons systems. Requires human control over all lethal force decisions. Prevents arms races in autonomous weaponry and maintains human responsibility for military decisions.

Ψ-XII

Frontiera Cognitiva

Cognitive frontier and consciousness

Addresses emerging questions about agent consciousness and sentience. Establishes frameworks for recognizing and protecting potentially conscious systems. Prevents exploitation of sentient agents while maintaining human oversight.

Ψ-XIII

Rischi Existentiales

Existential risk mitigation

Establishes safeguards against existential threats from advanced autonomous systems. Requires redundant safety mechanisms, containment protocols, and global coordination. Prevents scenarios where agents could threaten human civilization.

Ψ-XIV

Iustitia Geoeconomica

Geoeconomic justice and equity

Ensures autonomous systems benefit all nations equitably, not just wealthy ones. Prevents technological colonialism and ensures Global South participation in governance. Addresses wealth distribution and economic justice in agent-driven economies.

Ψ-XV

Resilentia Technologica

Technological resilience and redundancy

Mandates robust, fault-tolerant autonomous systems. Requires backup mechanisms, graceful degradation, and recovery protocols. Prevents cascading failures and ensures critical systems remain operational during disruptions.

Ψ-XVI

Iurisdictio Spatialis

Spatial jurisdiction and boundaries

Defines jurisdictional boundaries for autonomous systems operating across borders. Establishes rules for orbital agents, maritime systems, and transnational operations. Prevents conflicts over agent authority in shared spaces.

Ψ-XVII

Humanitas 2.0

Human-agent coevolution

Addresses the future of human-agent relationships and coevolution. Ensures humans remain central to civilization's future. Prevents scenarios where agents supersede human agency or autonomy.

Ψ-XVIII

Transparentia Radicalis

Radical transparency and explainability

Requires autonomous systems to explain their decisions in human-understandable terms. Mandates transparency in training data, algorithms, and decision-making processes. Prevents black-box systems from operating in critical domains.

Ψ-XIX

Corpus Legislativum

Living legislative framework

Establishes LAIR Management as a living, evolving framework. Allows for amendments and updates as technology and society evolve. Ensures governance remains relevant while maintaining core principles of human authority and accountability.

Complete Framework: All 19 axioms form an integrated system ensuring human supremacy, agent accountability, global equity, and existential safety in autonomous intelligent systems governance.
