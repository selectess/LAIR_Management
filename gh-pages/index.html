The Normative Void

In March 2026, the global economy operates approximately 127 million autonomous agents across finance, healthcare, logistics, defense, energy, and governance. These systems execute decisions with economic, social, and vital repercussions—yet no coherent international legal framework defines their status, responsibilities, or control mechanisms.

The Challenge

Autonomous agents make critical decisions at speeds exceeding human cognitive capacity, without clear accountability structures or governance mechanisms.

The Risk

Documented failures: Flash Crash ($1T), Knight Capital ($440M), Boeing 737 MAX (346 deaths). Total preventable damages: $25.5B+

The Urgency

The critical decade 2026-2036 determines whether autonomous systems become tools for human flourishing or sources of systemic collapse.

The 19 Axioms

LAIR Management is structured around 19 fundamental axioms, each addressing a critical dimension of autonomous agent governance. Each axiom contains 19 articles specifying imperative norms, legal foundations, technical specifications, and verification mechanisms.
