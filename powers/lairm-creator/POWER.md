## Project Identity

### What is LAIRM?

**LAIRM** (Legislature for Autonomous Intelligent Resources Management) is a Global Agentive Constitution establishing principles, norms, and control mechanisms to ensure autonomous AI systems operate in alignment with human values and the public interest.

**Validity Period**: 2026-2036 - The critical decade for establishing international standards

**Core Principle - The Cybernetic Criterion**: For any autonomous system operating in human society, there must exist verifiable mechanisms ensuring alignment with human values, accountability for decisions, and the capacity for human intervention when systems deviate from their intended purposes.

## Key Technical Specifications

### Universal Kill-Switch (Ψ-I)
- **Response time**: < 500ms
- **Channels**: Network, local, hardware (redundant)
- **Activation**: Competent human authority
- **Scope**: All agents or specific category

### Agent Passport (Ψ-II)
- **Format**: DID (W3C standard)
- **Components**: Unique ID, cryptographic keys, metadata
- **Storage**: Blockchain-based, immutable
- **Verification**: Cryptographic proof

### MCP Protocol (Ψ-V)
- **Purpose**: Agent-to-tool communication
- **Status**: 2,500+ registered servers (March 2026)
- **Standard**: Anthropic (November 2025)

### A2A Protocol (Ψ-V)
- **Purpose**: Agent-to-agent communication
- **Developers**: Google, OpenAI
- **Use case**: Multi-agent systems
